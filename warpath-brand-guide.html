Brand Foundation

Design Principles

Every visual choice serves a single mission: prove that Warpath Coffee is the smoothest, no-compromise cup on the table — roasted by veterans, brewed for anyone with standards. Six principles guide every decision, from package weight to button radius.

— Field Manual · Section 01 · Brand Operations —
Doctrine 001Mission First
M

No compromise on the cup.

The product carries the brand. Every layout, photo, and headline points back to one promise — smooth, low-acid, drink-it-black coffee. If a design choice doesn't make the cup look better, taste better, or sell better, it's cut.

Filed · Brand Lead— TG ·14·05·26
Doctrine 002Tactical Restraint
T

Brass is a signal, not a mood.

Brass appears for primary CTAs, italic accents, and the rule under the wordmark — never as a wash. Battle Red is reserved strictly for urgency: low stock, flash sales, "ships today." Combat black and bone canvas do the heavy lifting.

Filed · Brand Lead— TG ·14·05·26
Doctrine 003Earned Grit
E

Real texture, real weight.

Grain overlays. Coffee-stain marks. Stencil tags. Heavy display type that earns its space. Surfaces feel printed, not rendered. Polish is reserved for the product photography — everything around it can show its hand.

Filed · Brand Lead— TG ·14·05·26
Doctrine 004Veteran Respect
V

Service, not cosplay.

Founder is Navy SEAL combat veteran Tej Gill. The brand reflects that with quiet credibility — never costume eagles, never camo wallpaper, never "support our troops" graphics. The badge is earned by the work, not the typeface.

Filed · Brand Lead— TG ·14·05·26
Doctrine 005Cup-First Photo
C

The bean is the hero.

Bags, beans, brews, and roastery — that's the photo whitelist. People appear as supporting cast: a hand on the press, a steam-fogged window, a deployment patch on a sleeve. Stock handshakes and rifle-and-mug compositions do not exist here.

Filed · Brand Lead— TG ·14·05·26
Doctrine 006Built for Pour
B

CRO is the whole point.

This is a commerce site. Every page is engineered to move a visitor toward Subscribe, Add to Cart, or Repeat Order. Whitespace earns its place. Decoration that doesn't serve conversion is decoration that doesn't ship.

Filed · Brand Lead— TG ·14·05·26
Brand DNA: Tactical heritage meets craft-coffee editorial. Engineered like a field manual, dressed like a specialty roaster magazine — combat black surfaces, brass-cartridge accents, bone-canvas paper, and military-precise display type warmed by an editorial italic. Built for veterans, first responders, and anyone who refuses bitter coffee.

The Warpath mark — a heritage emblem rendered with combat-precision linework — locks above the wordmark "WARPATH" in Big Shoulders Black, set over a brass rule with the mono tagline "VETERAN-OWNED · DRINK IT BLACK". Accessibility

Every brand pairing meets WCAG 2.2 AA minimum; primary combinations hit AAA. The site is committed to equal experience regardless of ability, device, connection, or age. Customers with low vision, motor impairment, or screen-reader use complete checkout exactly as well as anyone else.

Accessibility Checklist

  • Body copy minimum 16px — never smaller for primary content
  • Line-height 1.65 for body, 0.92 for display
  • Interactive targets minimum 44×44px (touch)
  • Visible focus rings on every interactive element (2px brass outline, 2px offset)
  • Color never used as the sole signal — always paired with text, icon, or pattern
  • Alt text on every product image, mark, and meaningful visual; empty alt on decorative grain
  • Semantic HTML landmarks (header, nav, main, section, footer)
  • Skip-to-content link as first focusable element
  • Forms: labels associated, errors announced, hints visible, required fields marked
  • Cart and checkout fully keyboard-operable; no mouse-only flows
  • Motion respects prefers-reduced-motion — fades replace parallax, marquees pause, GSAP timelines collapse to instant state
  • Product gallery captions and audio descriptions for any embedded video
  • Tested with NVDA (Windows), VoiceOver (macOS/iOS), and TalkBack (Android)
  • Stripe / payment fields meet PCI-DSS accessibility plus AA minimum
Testing standard: Every release is audited against WCAG 2.2 AA using axe-core in CI, manual keyboard-only navigation, and NVDA/VoiceOver screen readers. AAA is targeted on all primary color pairs, hero headlines, body copy, and the entire checkout funnel. Failed audits block release — no exceptions. An accessible cart is a higher-converting cart.

Photography & Imagery

Photography is the second voice of the brand — documentary, grounded, product-led. The bag, the bean, the brew, the roastery, the steam off a fresh pour. Veterans appear as authentic supporting cast, never as costume props. Duotone (combat × brass) treatment is reserved for archival and editorial sections only.

Do & Don't

Do

  • Shoot in natural light — early morning kitchen, golden roastery floor, soft side-light on bag
  • Lead with product — bag, bean, brew, cup. People support, not dominate
  • Apply combat × brass duotone only to heritage / founder / archival imagery
  • Show real brew gear — Chemex, French press, AeroPress, drip — not hyper-stylized barista props
  • Capture steam, crema, grind texture, bean detail in macro
  • Leave negative space on the left or top for typographic overlays
  • Let grain stay — film texture, bean dust, kitchen-counter wear are on-brand

Don't

  • Use stock handshakes, salutes, waving flags, bald eagles, or rifle-and-mug compositions
  • Over-saturate, use HDR, apply aggressive sharpening, or any Instagram filter look
  • Crop product so the wordmark or roast level becomes unreadable
  • Mix duotone and full-color in the same grid, slider, or carousel
  • Add drop shadows, vignettes, neon glows, or bake text directly onto product photos
  • Use imagery featuring competing brands, generic agency backdrops, or AI-generated faces
  • Use camo wallpaper, tactical-cosplay props, or "warrior aesthetic" cliché

Tone of Voice

Voice & Messaging

Warpath sounds like a senior chief who happens to roast great coffee — confident, plain-spoken, no marketing mush. Short sentences. Earned claims. Zero hype words. The product is the proof; the copy gets out of its way.

Voice Pillars

C
Pillar 01 · WP-V01

Confident, not cocky.

Make claims you can defend in a tasting. "Drink it black, no sugar necessary" — not "world's smoothest cup." Specific beats superlative every time.

ToneEarned
P
Pillar 02 · WP-V02

Plain English. Period.

No "artisanal", "elevated", "experiential", "best-in-class". Say what it is. "Custom dark roast. Chocolate, almond, clean finish." Done.

LexiconStrict
S
Pillar 03 · WP-V03

Service earns the stripe.

Reference founder's service factually — "Founded by Navy SEAL combat veteran Tej Gill." Never wave it around as a marketing flag or sales prop.

HeritageQuiet
B
Pillar 04 · WP-V04

Bean first, story second.

Lead every PDP with taste, roast, brew method. The veteran-owned story lives on /our-story — it sells the second cup, not the first.

Digital Camo (Restricted Use)

Maximum one camo element per page — never as section background, never behind text

Camo is reserved for one purpose: a small accent on the "Operator Subscribe" tier card, the gift-card pattern, or a thin strip cap above flash-drop announcements. Never used as page wallpaper, never behind body copy, never on packaging artwork. The brand wears uniform, not costume.

Iconography rule of engagement: No more than two military elements per section, no more than one per hero. Stencil never sits next to Big Shoulders Display in the same headline. Patches and dog-tags appear on About / Story pages and account dashboards — not on PDPs. Camo never crosses 8% of any view's surface area. Coordinates appear in footers and order metadata only. The military touch should feel like rank tabs on a uniform — visible, structural, never decorative.

Key Rules

  • Lead with bone & combat — reserve brass for moments
  • Battle Red only for urgency (low stock, flash sale, error)
  • Never mix display + serif within one line
  • Break the grid deliberately, never accidentally
  • No drop shadows on type; gradients only on hero / bento / product tiles
  • No stock handshakes, eagles, rifles, camo wallpaper, or salutes
  • 16px body floor · WCAG AA minimum, AAA on primary pairs
  • Respect prefers-reduced-motion (pause marquees, collapse GSAP timelines)
  • Star glyph (★), chevron, and the Warpath mark are the only sanctioned ornaments
  • Military elements (stencil, dog-tag, patch, camo) — max 2 per section, max 1 per hero
  • Camo never exceeds 8% of any view; never behind body copy
  • Cart & Subscribe always above the fold on PDP and category pages
  • Plain English in copy — no "artisanal", "elevated", "experiential"
  • Founder's service stated factually, never used as marketing flag
